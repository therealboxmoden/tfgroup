What is Being Kind?

An intro to the Trans-Fem* Support Group

What is Trans-Fem*?

Trans–Fem* is a support, social, and resource group for transwomen, intersex, non-binary & questioning people who were assigned male at birth. We meet for fun, discussion & support around what it means to be on the trans-feminine spectrum. There are no requirements for transition status or presentation to attend this group. Our goals as a support group are to help each other heal from traumas, help each other find the resources we need, and promote social well-being by creating empathetic connections with each other.

What does it mean to be kind?

Our one broad group agreement is to “be kind.” This is a simple reminder to help facilitate the above stated group goals. Some examples of what being kind might look like include:

  • Pausing between people speaking. This helps people process and makes certain one person is done talking before another person begins. Don’t be afraid of silence.
  • Trying active listening and making more of your talk time responding to others contributions if you tend to talk a lot in groups.
  • Being bold: it’s okay to be vulnerable in our support group that’s why we meet.
  • Not worrying about the discussion “moving on”: if you want to go back to something, feel free to bring it back up especially if you tend to be quiet in group settings.
  • Speaking from personal experience rather than generalizing or giving second-hand information.
  • Avoiding side conversations and crosstalk.
  • Being present and showing that you value what others have to say.
  • Take care of yourself. Make accommodations and advocate for yourself if a discussion is upsetting to you.

By thinking about and implementing these ideas as you navigate your time at Trans-Fem* you can help yourself get support and help others get effective support as well. Thank you for joining us!
